Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal, Agra

One of the most famous historical sites in India, the Taj Mahal of Agra dates back to the 17th century. It was built by Shah Jahan, the Mughal emperor, in the memory of Mumtaz Mahal, his third wife. It is also the abode of the tomb of Shah Jahan himself.

Agra Fort, UP

A true example of the stunning Mughal form of architecture, the 16th century Agra Fort was built during Akbar's reign and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site as well.

Red Fort, Delhi

When it comes to the famous historical places in India, one cannot miss the Red Fort in Delhi. Also popularly known as the Lal Qila, the Red Fort was constructed during Shah Jahan's reign in the 17th century.

Qutub Minar, Delhi

Delhi's Qutub Minar ranks amongst the top 10 historical places in India. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Qutub Minar, also known as the Victory Tower, is the tallest brick minaret in the whole world, standing tall at a height of 72.5 metres.

Fatehpur Sikri, UP

An architectural gem and one of the most exquisite historical places in India is Fatehpur Sikri, a town in the Agra district of Uttar Pradesh.

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

Hawa Mahal in Jaipur is one of the best historical places in India, in addition to being a prominent landmark of Rajasthan. Dating back to the 18th century, Hawa Mahal is a 5-storey structure that overlooks the bustling streets of Jaipur.

Victoria Memorial

Kolkata's Victoria Memorial was constructed in honour of Queen Victoria, making it one of the relics of the British Raj. Built entirely out of white marble, the Victoria Memorial is almost a replica of its namesake in London.

Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb in Delhi was also the first garden-tomb in the country. Built by Emperor Humayun's wife in memory of her husband in the 16th century, this monument is surrounded by double-storied gateways and four gardens, and looks simply phenomenal.

Amer Fort, Jaipur

Amer Fort was built by Maharaja Man Singh in the 16th century, this fort is said to be amongst the most ancient monuments in the country. It boasts of intricately carved walls, opulent ceilings, multiple courtyards, as well as stunning entry gates.

India Gate, Delhi

Of all the famous historical places in India, India Gate possibly ranks at the top. An iconic landmark of Delhi, this sandstone-granite arch gate is amongst the largest war memorials in all of India, and is surrounded by verdant lawns and gardens.

Golden Temple, Punjab

Also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, the Golden temple is the most important religious site for the Sikhs, and celebrates humanity, tranquillity and brotherhood.

Mehrangarh Fort

Also known as Mehran Fort, the Mehrangarh Fort in Jodhpur is one of the most famous ancient places in India. Built by Rao Jodha, the founder of Jodhpur, in 1459, the fort is also one of the largest in the country, and is situated at an elevation of over 400 feet.
